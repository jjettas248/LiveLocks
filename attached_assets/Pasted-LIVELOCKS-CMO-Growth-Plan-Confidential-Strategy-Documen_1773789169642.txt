LIVELOCKS

CMO Growth Plan

Confidential Strategy Document

March 2026

Prepared for internal executive use only. Not for external distribution.

1. Executive Summary
LiveLocks is a premium sports intelligence and play-by-play analytics platform built to deliver decisive edge to serious sports bettors, fantasy players, and sports enthusiasts. The product operates on a freemium model with two paid tiers: Pro ($40/month) and All Sports ($65/month). Free users receive access to up to 15 plays per session, with strategic paywall friction introduced at plays 13 and 15 — the two moments of highest engagement and intent.

Distribution is anchored to two high-density communities: Whop, a digital marketplace favored by sports bettors and content creators, and The Hangar, a private community of engaged sports analytics enthusiasts. These channels serve as the primary acquisition and retention surfaces for LiveLocks.

This CMO Growth Plan outlines an integrated, 90-day marketing strategy designed to accelerate paid conversion, reduce churn, deepen community engagement, and establish LiveLocks as the default destination for serious sports intelligence. The plan spans brand positioning, funnel optimization, organic content systems, community amplification, paid acquisition, a 14-day sprint plan, and a KPI framework for measuring success.

The overarching thesis: LiveLocks does not compete on price — it competes on precision, predictive accuracy, and access. Our marketing must reflect that. Every touchpoint from first impression to upgrade prompt should communicate one idea: this is where serious players come to win.

2. Brand Positioning Strategy
2.1 Positioning Statement
LiveLocks is the intelligence layer for sports bettors and analysts who refuse to rely on gut instinct. Where other platforms offer noise, LiveLocks delivers signal — precise, real-time play analysis that transforms how professionals approach their game.

2.2 Core Brand Pillars
Precision Over Volume: LiveLocks curates high-confidence plays, not spray-and-pray picks. Every output is intentional.
Community-Validated Intelligence: The platform is built in and for communities like Whop and The Hangar — our positioning must honor that tribal authenticity.
Progressive Access: The freemium model is not a limitation — it is proof of product confidence. Users who reach plays 13 and 15 are already sold; the paywall closes the deal.
The Serious Player's Platform: Messaging should repel casual users and attract dedicated, outcome-driven sports analysts.
2.3 Competitive Differentiation
Most sports analytics tools are built for mass consumption — they prioritize breadth over depth and engagement over accuracy. LiveLocks inverts this model. By capping free access at 15 plays and introducing friction at exactly the moments of highest signal clarity (plays 13 and 15), LiveLocks demonstrates that its value is so high it must be protected. This creates perceived scarcity and product authority that no discount-first competitor can replicate.

2.4 Tone of Voice
Confident, direct, and data-forward. LiveLocks speaks like a sharp analyst, not a sales team. Copy should be economical, verifiable, and results-oriented. Avoid hyperbole. Let the product's track record do the persuading.

2.5 Visual Identity Guidelines
Dark, high-contrast aesthetic. Sports analytics commands authority, and the visual system should reflect that. Primary palette: deep navy and charcoal with electric accent (gold or green for positive signals, red for alerts). Typography should be modern sans-serif for data displays and strong serifs for editorial content.

3. Conversion Funnel Optimization
3.1 Funnel Architecture
The LiveLocks conversion funnel operates in five stages: Awareness → Activation → Play Consumption → Paywall Friction → Paid Conversion. Each stage requires deliberate design to minimize drop-off and maximize qualified conversion.

3.2 Awareness Stage
Awareness is driven through Twitter/X (Called Shot framework — detailed in Section 4), Whop marketplace listing optimization, The Hangar community touchpoints, and paid acquisition (Section 6). The awareness goal is not reach — it is reach among the right audience: bettors and analysts who already value data-driven decision making.

3.3 Activation Stage
First-session experience is critical. Free users must immediately feel the product's intelligence. Onboarding flow should: (1) frame the 15-play session as a curated intelligence briefing, not a free trial; (2) highlight the accuracy of past plays; (3) set the expectation that plays 13 and 15 represent the platform's sharpest calls.

3.4 Paywall Friction at Plays 13 and 15
The dual-paywall trigger is the platform's most powerful conversion mechanic. At play 13, users encounter a soft gate — a compelling upgrade prompt that previews the remaining plays and emphasizes what's at stake. At play 15 (the session cap), users hit a hard gate requiring upgrade to continue.

Optimization priorities for these moments:

Play 13 Prompt: Lead with the specific value of the locked plays — tease the sport, the edge, and the confidence level. Use urgency framing ('This window closes at game time').
Play 15 Gate: Make the upgrade CTA the only logical action. Display cumulative session accuracy, Pro vs. Free comparison, and a one-click upgrade path.
A/B Test: Test social proof variants ('4,200 Pro members made this call') against scarcity variants ('Only 3 plays remaining tonight').
3.5 Pricing Ladder Optimization
The Pro tier at $40/month and All Sports tier at $65/month represent a $25 upsell gap. Positioning All Sports as the 'serious player's choice' while presenting Pro as the 'starter edge' creates a natural upgrade journey. Consider introducing an annual pricing option (Pro Annual at $320, All Sports Annual at $520) to increase LTV and reduce monthly churn risk.

3.6 Churn Recovery
Cold reactivation is a high-ROI lever. Users who previously subscribed and churned are the warmest re-acquisition target. Segment churned users by tier (Pro vs. All Sports) and days since churn (<30, 30-90, 90+). Deploy personalized win-back sequences via email and direct message through Whop's messaging system, featuring their last session's results and what they've missed since churning.

4. Organic Content Engine
4.1 The Called Shot Framework (Twitter/X)
The Called Shot framework is LiveLocks' signature organic content system. Modeled on high-accountability sports prediction culture, Called Shot posts publicly commit to specific analytical positions before outcomes are determined — then follow up with documented results. This creates compounding credibility over time and generates viral moments when calls hit.

Called Shot post structure:

Pre-game: [Sport] | [Team/Player] | [Specific Edge or Prediction] | Confidence Level: HIGH/ELITE | #CalledShot @LiveLocks
Post-game: [Result summary] | Called Shot confirmed/adjusted | [What the data said] | [Replay link or screenshot]
Called Shot content should be posted consistently — minimum 3 per day across key sports windows (early afternoon, pre-primetime, late night). Volume and consistency build the archive that turns skeptics into believers over 30-60 days.

4.2 Content Calendar Framework
Organize content into four weekly content types:

Monday: Performance Recap — Previous week's Called Shot results, win rates, and notable calls
Tuesday/Thursday: Deep Analysis — Long-form breakdown of a specific matchup, trend, or analytical system
Wednesday/Friday: Live Call Thread — Real-time play-by-play commentary during high-profile games
Saturday/Sunday: Weekend Preview — Top plays for the weekend's biggest matchups, with Pro/All Sports content previews
4.3 Community Content Amplification
Incentivize Pro and All Sports members to share results publicly. Build a 'Results Reel' program where users can submit their wins derived from LiveLocks plays. Feature top submissions in weekly content. This generates authentic social proof and turns satisfied subscribers into distribution partners.

4.4 SEO and Discovery Content
Publish weekly analytical long-form content targeting high-intent search terms: '[Sport] advanced analytics,' '[Team] betting edge,' '[Player] performance metrics.' Each article should contain a natural LiveLocks CTA and link to the platform's top play of the week — demonstrating product capability in context.

5. Community Growth
5.1 Whop Marketplace Strategy
Whop is a primary acquisition channel and should be treated as a storefront, community hub, and social proof engine simultaneously. Optimization priorities:

Listing Optimization: Lead with results-first copy. Feature Called Shot accuracy rates, notable wins, and member count prominently. Use professional visual assets consistent with the LiveLocks brand identity.
Review Velocity: Actively solicit reviews from satisfied Pro and All Sports members within the first 7 days of subscription. Offer a bonus play analysis or exclusive community access as an incentive for verified reviews.
Whop Affiliate Program: Activate and optimize the Whop affiliate system. Identify top referrers within The Hangar and Whop community and offer tiered commission structures (15% standard, 20% for top 10 affiliates).
Whop Drops and Promotions: Use Whop's promotional tools to run limited-time offers during major sports events (Super Bowl, March Madness, playoff runs). Position these as 'Intelligence Events,' not discount sales.
5.2 The Hangar Community Strategy
The Hangar is a retention and engagement accelerator. It is the place where LiveLocks members experience the product's full value — shared plays, community discussion, and collective wins. Growth priorities:

Exclusivity Positioning: The Hangar should feel earned. Pro members access The Hangar as a core benefit. Frame it as the inner circle, not a bonus.
Daily Engagement Hooks: Post one exclusive play or analytical insight in The Hangar daily that is not available through the standard product. This creates habitual return behavior.
Member Spotlights: Feature member wins and testimonials weekly. Public recognition drives community pride and increases word-of-mouth referrals from within.
Cross-Promotion: Encourage Hangar members to share Called Shot content and tag LiveLocks. Coordinate simultaneous posting during high-traffic sports windows to amplify organic reach.
5.3 Community-Led Referral System
Launch a structured referral program: Pro members receive a personalized referral link. Each successful referral that converts to any paid tier earns the referring member one free month of service. All Sports referrals earn two free months. This aligns member incentives with growth objectives and creates a viral loop anchored in existing high-intent users.

6. Paid Acquisition
6.1 Meta Advertising Strategy
Meta (Facebook and Instagram) is the primary paid acquisition channel due to its advanced interest targeting capabilities and the ability to build lookalike audiences from existing subscriber lists.

6.2 Audience Architecture
Cold Audiences: Sports betting interests, fantasy sports enthusiasts, DFS players, sports analytics communities, adjacent financial and data-driven decision-making audiences
Warm Audiences: Website visitors, free users who did not convert, video viewers (50%+ of Called Shot video content), Whop page visitors
Hot Audiences: Churned subscribers (cold reactivation), email list subscribers, Whop purchaser lookalikes
6.3 Meta Retargeting Framework
Retargeting is the highest-ROI paid channel for LiveLocks. Structure retargeting campaigns in three tiers:

Tier 1 — Play 13 Abandoners: Users who reached the play 13 paywall but did not upgrade. Serve urgency-led creative featuring the specific plays they were locked out of. 48-hour remarket window.
Tier 2 — Play 15 Hard Gate Abandoners: Users who hit the session cap and exited. Serve social proof-led creative featuring member results. 7-day remarket window.
Tier 3 — Cold Reactivation: Churned Pro and All Sports subscribers. Serve personalized win-back creative featuring season-to-date accuracy data and a time-limited return offer. 90-day window.
6.4 Creative Strategy
Top-performing creative formats for sports analytics audience:

Results Screenshots: Before/after screen captures of Called Shot predictions and outcomes. High authenticity, high trust-building.
Video Testimonials: 30-45 second member testimonial videos shot on mobile. Native-feeling, platform-appropriate.
Data Visualization Ads: Clean, branded stat cards showing win rates, prediction accuracy, and notable calls. High shareability.
Direct Response Copy: Short, punchy ad copy leading with a specific win ('This call paid 4x last Tuesday. Here's why we made it.').
6.5 Budget Allocation
Recommended initial monthly paid budget allocation: 40% cold audience prospecting, 35% warm audience retargeting (Tier 1 and 2), 25% cold reactivation (Tier 3 and churned subscriber recovery). Shift budget dynamically based on ROAS performance by tier. Target blended ROAS of 3.5x in months 1-2, scaling to 5x+ as creative and audience data mature.

6.6 Attribution and Measurement
Implement UTM tracking across all paid campaigns. Connect Whop purchase data to Meta Conversions API to improve attribution accuracy in a cookieless environment. Track cost per free registration, cost per play 13 reach, cost per paid conversion, and cost per tier (Pro vs. All Sports) as primary efficiency metrics.

7. 14-Day Sprint Plan
Days 1-2: Foundation
Audit all existing social profiles, Whop listing, and The Hangar community for brand consistency. Standardize copy, imagery, and CTA language.
Set up UTM tracking infrastructure across all acquisition channels.
Finalize Called Shot content calendar for days 3-14.
Draft and schedule 6 Called Shot posts (pre-game) for the first week of sports matchups.
Days 3-4: Content and Community Activation
Publish first Called Shot thread on Twitter/X. Monitor engagement and respond to all replies within 2 hours.
Post first exclusive Hangar-only analytical insight. Announce Results Reel program to existing members.
Optimize Whop marketplace listing with new results-first copy and updated visual assets.
Identify top 5 Whop affiliates and initiate direct outreach with affiliate program offer.
Days 5-7: Paid Launch
Launch Meta cold audience prospecting campaign with two creative variants (results screenshot vs. data visualization).
Launch play 13 abandoner retargeting campaign (Tier 1). Install Meta Pixel and Conversions API if not already active.
Begin A/B test on play 13 paywall prompt copy: urgency variant vs. social proof variant.
Days 8-10: Optimization Cycle
Review Days 3-7 performance data. Pause underperforming Meta creative. Scale winning variants.
Publish first long-form SEO article with LiveLocks CTA embedded naturally.
Send first cold reactivation sequence to churned subscribers (<30 days since churn). Segment by prior tier.
Post week 1 Called Shot performance recap: document accuracy rate and notable wins publicly.
Days 11-12: Referral and Community Growth
Launch member referral program to existing Pro and All Sports subscribers via Whop messaging and email.
Publish first member spotlight in The Hangar and on Twitter/X.
Activate play 15 hard gate abandoner retargeting (Tier 2). Test social proof creative.
Days 13-14: Synthesis and 30-Day Planning
Compile sprint performance report: conversion rate changes at plays 13 and 15, Meta ROAS by campaign, community engagement metrics, referral program early results.
Identify top 3 channels by cost-per-conversion and highest-performing creative for scale.
Draft 30-day plan based on sprint learnings. Set Month 1 targets for paid subscribers, Whop reviews, and Called Shot follower growth.
8. KPIs and Success Metrics
8.1 North Star Metrics
Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR): Total paid subscription revenue. Target: 20% month-over-month growth in months 1-3.
Paid Conversion Rate: Percentage of free users who upgrade to Pro or All Sports. Baseline to be established in sprint week 1; target 8-12%.
Net Revenue Retention (NRR): Combined effect of expansion (Free→Pro→All Sports upgrades) and churn. Target NRR >110%.
8.2 Acquisition Metrics
Cost Per Free Registration (CPFR): Meta spend / new free user registrations. Target: <$4.00 per free registration.
Cost Per Paid Acquisition (CPPA): Total paid marketing spend / new paid subscribers. Target: <$35 blended (across both tiers).
Whop Listing Click-Through Rate: Optimize to >4% from marketplace browse impressions.
Called Shot Follower Growth: Target 500 new Twitter/X followers per month in months 1-3, accelerating to 1,000+ in month 4+.
8.3 Conversion Funnel Metrics
Play 13 Upgrade Rate: % of users who reach play 13 and convert to paid. Track weekly. Target: 15%+.
Play 15 Upgrade Rate: % of users who hit the session cap and convert within 24 hours. Target: 25%+.
A/B Test Conversion Lift: Measure urgency vs. social proof variants at paywall. Document winning variant and implement at day 10.
8.4 Retention and Engagement Metrics
Monthly Churn Rate: Target <7% for Pro tier, <5% for All Sports tier.
Cold Reactivation Rate: Percentage of churned subscribers who resubscribe within 90-day window. Target: 10%+.
The Hangar Daily Active Participation Rate: Members who engage with at least one post per week. Target: 40%+ of active members.
Referral Program Conversion: Referred users who convert to paid. Track by referring member tier.
8.5 Content Performance Metrics
Called Shot Accuracy Rate: Public win rate on Called Shot predictions. Document and publish monthly. Minimum acceptable threshold: 58% (industry benchmark for credibility).
Engagement Rate per Called Shot Post: Target average engagement rate of 3%+ (likes + replies + reposts / impressions).
SEO Content Organic Traffic: Monthly unique visitors from organic search to LiveLocks content. Target: 500 in month 1, scaling to 2,500 by month 3.
8.6 Reporting Cadence
Weekly: Paid campaign performance, conversion rates at plays 13 and 15, Called Shot content metrics, Whop listing traffic and reviews. Monthly: MRR, churn, NRR, referral program results, full funnel conversion report, and 30-day plan review. Quarterly: Full CMO strategy review, pricing analysis, community health audit, and competitive landscape reassessment.

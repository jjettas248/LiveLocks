Red flag #1: Directional skew

Your recent plays are overwhelmingly UNDERS. A healthy engine can lean one way on a given slate, but when the model is repeatedly generating mostly one direction across many players and markets, that usually means a systematic bias in expected value generation, not just noisy late-season rotations.

Red flag #2: Probability/edge mismatch in persisted plays

This is the more serious issue.

In the persisted table, you have entries like:

Dir = U

Prob = 16%–39%

Edge = +13pp to +33pp

That combination is logically inconsistent unless the stored probability is the opposite side’s probability or the edge is being computed against the wrong directional confidence.

Example: if the pick is UNDER, the surfaced confidence should generally be the under confidence, not 16% or 31%. If you are storing the displayed Prob as one side and computing Edge from the other side, the engine can look massively under-heavy and “profitable” on paper while actually being mis-specified in output formatting or downstream persistence.

That is the first thing I would audit.

Red flag #3: The miss pattern is clustered, not random

Your misses are not evenly distributed. They cluster around:

star unders

volatile role/bench players

combo unders on players with spiky scoring profiles

That is a structural pattern, not just late-season chaos.

What this likely means

You probably have two overlapping issues:

1. Real model under-bias

Your projection stack is likely still too low on:

superstars

high-usage closers

volatile microwave scorers

That would naturally create too many unders.

2. Output-side directional bug

Your persisted Prob/Edge values strongly suggest a directional confidence inversion or mismatch somewhere between:

calculateProbability()

response shaping

persistence to the new table

analytics rendering

That needs to be checked before you tune the model further.

This is why I would audit first

In sports modeling, calibration matters more than raw “accuracy”; a 70% bucket should behave like a 70% bucket over time, otherwise the probabilities are not trustworthy.

If you start layering more filters before confirming that:

under probability is the same value used for edge

persisted probability matches surfaced probability

analytics bucketing uses directional confidence consistently

you risk tuning around a plumbing bug instead of a model problem.

My verdict

The engine is off, but I would not blame end-of-season variance first.
I would assign the causes roughly like this:

60–70%: structural under-bias + superstar/volatile-player handling

20–30%: late-season rotation volatility

10–20%: possible persistence/rendering mismatch on directional probability/edge
